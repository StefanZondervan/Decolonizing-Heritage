Silent Connection
Among the many rituals that speak to our shared spiritual heritage, the Abhishek — the sacred act of bathing a deity — holds a special place. Traditionally performed by pouring water, milk, or other offerings over a Lingam, it is not about cleansing the divine, for the divine is pure. Instead, it is a symbolic act of cleansing oneself — of purifying the heart, mind, and soul. Years ago, I gave my father a Lingam as a gift. It may seem simple, but for me, this gesture carries profound meaning. Throughout my life, my relationship with my father has been marked by few words and little direct connection. We rarely speak, and when we do, our conversations are brief. But this object — this sacred symbol — has showed a bridge between us. In its presence, I feel we are connected at a deeper level, beyond the need for many words. It represents not only spiritual devotion but also an unspoken bond, a quiet understanding that perhaps was always there...

L khamsa 🪬
This necklace is very special to me. It was given to me by my grandmother, and it carries a deep meaning from our Tunisian culture. It has five small hands, which are known as the ‘Khamsa’ or the Hand of Fatima. In our tradition, the five hands are believed to protect us from the evil eye and keep us safe. My grandmother gave me this necklace as a way to protect me, especially when I’m far from her. It’s not just jewelry it’ s a symbol of her love, our culture, and the spiritual protection she wants me to have wherever I go.
