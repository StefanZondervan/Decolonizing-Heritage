Family heritage
An important part of my mental heritage is the story of my great-grandfather, who fled from Russia during a time of political tension and instability. Together with my grandfather and his two brothers, he passed through several countries, leaving everything behind in search of safety and better life, eventually settling in Bulgaria. His journey, filled with uncertainty and hardship has been passed down to me through family stories.Even though I never met him, his decisions and sacrifices are a core part of me and my families identity.

Chill and vibe with life
A part of my mental heritage is the idea of aylyak — a Plovdiv state of mind that’s all about taking it easy and enjoying life. It’s more than being lazy — it’s about slowing down, being present, and finding joy in simple things. I grew up around this vibe: no one rushing, everything had its time, and there was always space for laughter, food, and connection. It taught me not to stress too much and to appreciate the moment. Whether you call it aylyak or just chilling with your friends, it’s a way of life I carry with me.
